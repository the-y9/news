    History¶

    Headline: Evolution of the Union Budget Tradition in India¶

    • Origin of "Budget": Derived from the French word "Bougette", meaning a leather briefcase.
    • The "Gladstone Box": In 1860, British Budget Chief William E. Gladstone used a red suitcase due to the extraordinary length of his speech, starting the briefcase tradition.
    • Independent India's First Budget: Presented by R.K. Shanmukham Chetty in 1947 using a leather portfolio bag.
    • Shift from Colonial Legacy: In 2019, Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman replaced the briefcase with the "Bahi Khata" (Sukh-Shanti pouch). By 2021, the budget transitioned to a Digital Tablet format.
    • Historical Milestones:
      • Morarji Desai holds the record for presenting the most budgets (10 times).
      • Manmohan Singh delivered the longest budget speech in terms of word count (18,650 words in 1991).
      • Yashwant Sinha changed the budget presentation time from 5 PM to 11 AM.
      • Arun Jaitley shifted the date to February 1st and merged the Railway Budget with the Union Budget in 2017.

    Polity¶

    Headline: Constitutional Framework and Parliamentary Process of the Budget¶

    • Article 112:ancial Statement (AFS); the word "Budget" i The Constitution refers to the Budget as the Annual Fins not mentioned in the Constitution.
    • Preparation: Prepared by the Budget Division under the Department of Economic Affairs, Ministry of Finance.
    • Six Stages in Parliament: Presentation, General Discussion, Scrutiny by Departmental Committees, Voting on Demand for Grants, passing of the Appropriation Bill, and finally the Finance Bill.
    • Halwa Ceremony: A traditional ritual marking the commencement of the budget printing process (conducted in the North Block basement) followed by a "lock-in" period for officials to maintain confidentiality.
    • Finance Commission: The government accepted the 16th Finance Commission's recommendation for a 41% vertical devolution of the divisible tax pool to State Governments.

    Economy¶

    Headline: Union Budget 2026-27: Key Fiscal Indicators and Sectoral Allocations¶

    • Fiscal Parameters:
      • Total Estimated Receipts: ₹53.4 Lakh Crore.
      • Total Estimated Expenditure: ₹58.4 Lakh Crore.
      • Debt-to-GDP Ratio: Target set at 50% (+/- 1%) by 2030 (currently 56.1%).
    • Revenue vs. Capital:
      • Capital Expenditure (CapEx): Targeted at ₹12.2 Lakh Crore for 2026-27 (Growth-oriented).
      • Revenue Expenditure: Estimated at ₹41.3 Lakh Crore (Consumption-oriented, including salaries and subsidies).
    • MSME Support:
      • TREDS (Trade Receivable e-Discounting System): Mandatory transaction settlement for MSMEs to ensure timely payments from CPSEs.
      • SME Growth Fund: A dedicated fund of ₹100 Crore.
      • Corporate Mitra: An institution to provide professional and affordable regulatory compliance support for small businesses.
    • Taxation: No major changes in income tax slabs. Relief provided via Customs Duty exemptions for certain cancer medicines and raw materials for the aircraft industry.
    • Infrastructure: Planning of 7 High-Speed Rail Corridors (e.g., Mumbai-Pune, Pune-Hyderabad, Hyderabad-Bengaluru) and development of Temple Towns.

    Science & Tech¶

    Headline: Technological Missions and Bio-Pharma Research¶

    • India Semiconductor Mission 2.0: Focus on boosting domestic semiconductor manufacturing.
    • Bio-Pharma Shakti: A new initiative for research and development in the pharmaceutical sector.
    • Rare Earth Permanent Magnets: New schemes to incentivize the production of critical components.

    Defense¶

    Headline: Significant Hike in Defense Outlay¶

    • Allocation: The Defense budget has been increased by 15%, reaching ₹7.85 Lakh Crore for 2026-27.

    Education¶

    Headline: Education Budget Allocation 2026-27¶

    • Spending: Allocated ₹1.39 Lakh Crore, a marginal increase from the previous year’s ₹1.28 Lakh Crore.

    Agriculture¶

    Headline: Focus on Cash Crops and Agro-Development¶

    • Coconut Development: Special focus on the development of coconut farming following recent disease outbreaks (e.g., Root Wilt disease).
    • Import Substitution: Efforts to increase domestic production of Cashew and Agarwood to reduce import dependency.
