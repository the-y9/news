    International Relations¶

    Headline: India successfully tests Solid Fuel Ducted Ramjet (SFDR) technology, enhancing air-to-air missile capabilities.¶

    • India, in collaboration with Russia, has successfully tested the Solid Fuel Ducted Ramjet (SFDR) technology.
    • This technology is a missile propulsion system that uses a ramjet engine, an air-breathing jet engine that compresses incoming air to create high pressure for combustion.
    • The SFDR technology will enhance the combat effectiveness and range of India's long-range air-to-air missiles.
    • Missiles equipped with SFDR technology fly at supersonic speeds, are highly maneuverable, and difficult to intercept as they obtain oxygen from the atmosphere.
    • This technological advancement places India among a select few countries possessing this capability.

    Headline: Supreme Court questions WhatsApp on data privacy and consent practices.¶

    • The Supreme Court has raised concerns regarding WhatsApp's data access and the clarity of its consent mechanisms for users.
    • The court questioned how advertisements related to personal chats appear, implying a potential breach of privacy.
    • WhatsApp's claim of end-to-end encryption was challenged, with the court asking for simplified consent language understandable to the common person.
    • The court emphasized that user consent for data access does not grant permission for commercial exploitation, sale, or purchase of personal data.
    • The Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, and Article 21 (Right to Privacy) were highlighted as crucial legal frameworks governing data protection in India.
    • The court warned that if Meta, WhatsApp's parent company, violates the right to privacy, it may not be allowed to operate in India.

    • Reports indicate significant underutilization of funds by some Members of Parliament, particularly in Delhi, where substantial amounts remain unspent and carry forward.
    • Allegations of corruption, including non-existent projects, fake claims, and the involvement of single vendors potentially linked to MPs, have surfaced.
    • There is a mandatory provision for MPs to allocate a minimum of 15% of their funds for projects benefiting Scheduled Castes and 7.5% for Scheduled Tribes.
    • Nominated Rajya Sabha MPs can recommend projects anywhere in India, while elected Rajya Sabha MPs can recommend projects within their state, and Lok Sabha MPs within their constituency.

    • The scheme is non-lapsable, meaning unused funds carry forward to the next financial year, accumulating over the MP's tenure.

    Headline: NAMASTE Scheme aims to mechanize sanitation work and eliminate manual scavenging.¶

    • The National Action for Mechanized Sanitation Ecosystem (NAMASTE) Scheme, a joint initiative of the Ministry of Social Justice and Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs, aims to mechanize sanitation work.
    • Launched in 2022, the scheme's primary objective is to replace manual scavenging with machine-based cleaning.
    • Profiling of sanitation workers in urban areas is being conducted to identify those engaged in waste picking and sanitation.
    • Data reveals that a significant majority (84%) of identified sanitation workers belong to Scheduled Caste, Scheduled Tribe, and Other Backward Classes communities.
    • Some states like Delhi and Goa show a higher proportion of sanitation workers from the General Category.
    • The scheme is implemented by the National Safai Karamchari Finance and Development Corporation and aims to provide health insurance, safety measures, and rehabilitation to sanitation workers.

    Headline: Supreme Court's approach to death penalty cases highlights concerns over judicial adherence to guidelines.¶

    • The Supreme Court has not confirmed any death penalty sentences in the last three years, though lower courts continue to award them.
    • Lower courts are observed to frequently award the death penalty, often without strictly adhering to the "rarest of rare" doctrine established by the Supreme Court.
    • In 2023, lower courts sentenced 128 individuals to death, but the confirmation rate by High Courts and the Supreme Court remains very low.
    • The Supreme Court, in cases like Bachan Singh vs. State of Punjab (1980), has laid down guidelines for awarding the death penalty, including the "rarest of rare" principle, requiring brutal murders, depraved motives, and significant crimes.
    • Guidelines also stipulate a minimum 15-day gap between conviction and sentencing to allow for psychological preparation, which is often overlooked by subordinate courts.
    • The constitutionality of the death penalty has been upheld, but its application remains a subject of debate due to concerns about fairness, deterrence, and human rights.

    Headline: US-India trade deal details remain unclear; tariff on steel and penalty on oil imports reduced.¶

    • Specific details regarding the US-India trade deal are still pending clarification, with the Commerce Minister stating that more information will be available upon signing.
    • While the trade deal has been announced, it has not yet been formally signed.
    • A significant development is the reduction of the 25% penalty on India's oil imports from Russia to zero.
    • The tariff on steel has been reduced from 25% to 18%.
    • The impact of these tariff reductions on India's exports, particularly in sectors like leather and carpets, is expected to be positive.
    • The concept of "reciprocal tariffs" as employed by the Trump administration, where the US imposed tariffs based on tariffs levied by other countries, was discussed.
    • Government assurances indicate that farmers and exporters have been considered, and strategic sectors will be protected.

    • The future of industrial growth lies in transitioning from fossil fuels (oil, coal, gas) to clean electricity, referred to as "electrons."
    • Countries that embrace this shift will reduce emissions, enhance supply chain control, create jobs, and attract capital.
    • China's rapid industrial electrification and significant investment in renewable energy (solar, hydro, nuclear) are cited as examples of successful transition.
    • Reliable and clean electricity supply is critical for industries, and India needs to focus on ensuring 24/7 power availability to its industrial sectors to become a global manufacturing hub.
    • The transition to clean energy is presented not just as an environmental imperative but also a developmental and strategic necessity to reduce import dependency and achieve energy autonomy.
    • The article suggests that while initial costs of clean energy technologies are high, increasing demand and production, potentially including hydrogen, can lead to cost reduction and greater accessibility.

    • The successful test of Solid Fuel Ducted Ramjet (SFDR) technology by India marks a significant advancement in missile propulsion.
    • This technology is crucial for developing next-generation air-to-air missiles with extended range and superior maneuverability.

    • This development is a result of collaborative efforts, likely involving international partnerships, and strengthens India's defense technological capabilities.
